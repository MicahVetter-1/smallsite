ABOUT LEON

Leon Bridges is an American Soul singer songwriter from Fort Worth, Texas. Growing up in a split family, Bridges developed a very shy and sheepish personality at a very young age. Due to his low-confidence, he was not aware of his musical tallent until his early adult years where he learned to play the guitar at a local community college. As he began to develop his music style, many fans compared him to Sam Cooke- an American soul singer in the 50's. For several years, Leon's career was at a standstill, with him only attracting 20 people to his shows at a time. His big break came when Austin Jenkins sat in on his performance of "Coming Home". It was this song that catapulted Leon into stardom.

Leon has a very unique style. His look mashes up vintage pieces with things sourced from the '80s and '90s—he's got a freewheeling, omnivorous vibe. At first many people thought that his style wasn't a real depiction of him as a person. However, Leon says, "I think people now are seeing that it's authentic." His inspirations run deep: The singer was moved to start dressing the way he does after he discovered Street Etiquette, the avant-garde style blog founded and run by Joshua Kissi and Travis Gumbs.

Over the years, Leon Bridges has had several successful collaborations with fellow artists and singers. Perhaps the most well-known collaboration was with Luke Combs. Luke and Leon have now released two recordings from the performance: Luke’s “Beautiful Crazy” featuring Leon, and Leon’s “Beyond” featuring Luke. Since then, Leon has worked with artists such as Macklemore and Ryan Lewis.
